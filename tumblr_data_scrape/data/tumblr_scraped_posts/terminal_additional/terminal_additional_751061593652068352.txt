It <i>is</i> a photograph of his soul. </p><p>And that&rsquo;s precisely why it&rsquo;s so ugly and uncomfortable to look at. </p><div class="npf_row"><figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-height="1405" data-orig-width="1000"><img src="https://64.media.tumblr.com/cd03efd23833c1fd0fb6eff1edafb01d/d736f6df1b489db7-3c/s640x960/0859bd7c83c1c19effe8c51c24bb47a62382ddb2.jpg" data-orig-height="1405" data-orig-width="1000" srcset="https://64.media.tumblr.com/cd03efd23833c1fd0fb6eff1edafb01d/d736f6df1b489db7-3c/s75x75_c1/d2ad669889743f7a79b4c1005e2c31a6633302c8.jpg 75w, https://64.media.tumblr.com/cd03efd23833c1fd0fb6eff1edafb01d/d736f6df1b489db7-3c/s100x200/30006778543029f19d5c7008b1dad74edf65ac49.jpg 100w, https://64.media.tumblr.com/cd03efd23833c1fd0fb6eff1edafb01d/d736f6df1b489db7-3c/s250x400/b265a02dfcec797cb1fd846b697246b87d5d6676.jpg 250w, https://64.media.tumblr.com/cd03efd23833c1fd0fb6eff1edafb01d/d736f6df1b489db7-3c/s400x600/b127f2a29a183b81de87291fbc16d334e0da80cf.jpg 400w, https://64.media.tumblr.com/cd03efd23833c1fd0fb6eff1edafb01d/d736f6df1b489db7-3c/s500x750/2f5bf32e0c5efea5e19bf71c2434ce93e4b42cbd.jpg 500w, https://64.media.tumblr.com/cd03efd23833c1fd0fb6eff1edafb01d/d736f6df1b489db7-3c/s540x810/d3eb6ba9afd3d3978c504211a710976fcbc7e81d.jpg 540w, https://64.media.tumblr.com/cd03efd23833c1fd0fb6eff1edafb01d/d736f6df1b489db7-3c/s640x960/0859bd7c83c1c19effe8c51c24bb47a62382ddb2.jpg 640w, https://64.media.tumblr.com/cd03efd23833c1fd0fb6eff1edafb01d/d736f6df1b489db7-3c/s1280x1920/21bc201b448a8ede2dd024b8907aee062744d13f.jpg 1000w" sizes="(max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px"/></figure></div><p>People have said that Charles has a &ldquo;complicated legacy&rdquo;, which is what people say when someone has an objectively horrible legacy that they are still personally benefiting from. But the people who still tolerate his extravagant gilded existence to &ldquo;honor historical tradition&rdquo; will find absolutely <i>nothing</i> to like in this portrait. All the gold and brass and pomp of his uniform, all the military accolades for his colonial warmongering, all the fabulous ostentatious wealth he was born into and has spent every second of his life surrounded by - which would have been rendered with glittering precision and care in a traditional royal portrait - they&rsquo;re all dingy and washed out and already fading. The medals aren&rsquo;t even clearly marked enough to really know what they are; it&rsquo;s all sound and fury, signifying nothing.</p><p>The butterfly that was included as a nod to his honestly extensive conservation work (because let&rsquo;s give the little bit of credit where credit is actually due) stands out as the one bright point of beauty and authenticity - but it&rsquo;s dwarfed by the only other visible object, the sword, and it&rsquo;s being swallowed up by that lurid, putrid background that seems to seep out of Charles&rsquo; uniform. The dark tips of its wings are the most high-contrast part of the painting except for Charles&rsquo; black hollow eyes that stare into nothing. And, most significantly in my opinion, <b>the butterfly isn&rsquo;t actually touching him</b>, or connected to him in any way. It just exists alongside him, but it doesn&rsquo;t <i>need</i> him.</p><p>His face is painted in such a way to detail absolutely every wrinkle without ever being able to completely cover up the blood red background, and below the sunken shark-like eyes, the artist has included that vapidly pleasant plastered-on smile with nothing behind it that is practically the royal uniform by now. I think the angle is also deliberately chosen to be unsettling: many portraits are traditionally done either head-on, &frac34; profile, or full profile. Charles is none of these - his head is tilted juuust a few degrees off kilter. It&rsquo;s not quite right. And he&rsquo;s looking off to the side very slightly; his thousand-yard-stare is kind of drifting over the viewers shoulder. <b>He can&rsquo;t look us in the eye.</b> </p><p>And there is no way, there is <i>absolutely no possible way</i> that an artist who is smart enough and skilled enough to imbue all his other portraits with so much meaning and symbolism and indicators of the subject&rsquo;s character - there&rsquo;s no way that&rsquo;s not intentional.</p><p>But&hellip; Yeo lives in London. He&rsquo;s still working on other royal and aristocratic portraits. <b>He still has to live in that society, and he still has to get paid.</b></p><p>So of course he has to toe the line, at least until Charles dies, and say that the vivid blood-soaked red is to symbolize the &ldquo;&rdquo;&ldquo;vibrancy&rdquo;&ldquo;&rdquo; of this terminally ill octogenarian, to bring a &ldquo;&rdquo;&ldquo;modern contemporary feel&rdquo;&ldquo;&rdquo; to this 19th century colonizer. </p><p><b>Yeo knows <i>exactly</i> what he&rsquo;s doing.</b></p><p>Here&rsquo;s an excerpt about it from <a href="https://www.smithsonianmag.com/smart-news/is-king-charles-iiis-first-official-portrait-too-red-180984364/#:~:text=The%20intense%20red%20color%20that,contrast%20to%20more%20traditional%20depictions.%E2%80%9D" target="_blank">Smithsonian magazine</a>:</p><blockquote class="npf_indented"><p>The king saw the painting when it was about halfway done. Yeo tells BBC News’ Katie Razzall that Charles was “mildly surprised by the strong color, but otherwise he seemed to be smiling approvingly.” He adds that when Camilla saw the portrait, she said, “Yes, you’ve got him.”</p></blockquote><p>Listen, I work in memory care and end-of-life care, and we only say someone &ldquo;seems to be smiling approvingly&rdquo; to comfort the family when someone is so far gone they clearly don&rsquo;t know where they are anymore. His ex-wife Camilla, who probably has more good reasons to hate him than any other single human being alive, looked at this haunting vision of hell and was like YES PERFECT.</p><p><b>This is all completely intentional.</b> We are all picking up on <i>exactly</i> the message the artist was trying to convey. Yeo is trying to tell us, loud and clear, that something is not right here. It is <i>absolutely</i> an omen.</p><p>Op is right; it <i>is</i> insulting him.
